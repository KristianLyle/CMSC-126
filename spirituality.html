Animism

Animism is the belief that all objects, places, and creatures, including humans, possess a distinct spiritual essence. This belief system was prevalent among precolonial Filipinos, who believed that there were spirits and supernatural entities in nature that controlled and influenced various aspects of their lives. These spirits were known as anito, diwata, nuno, and other similar terms.

Hinduism

Precolonial Filipinos were influenced by Hindu culture and religion through trade contacts with India and Indonesia, which led to the adoption of Hindu concepts and deities. Among the concepts adopted were karma, which means actions have consequences, and dharma, which refers to the natural order of the universe.

Buddhism

Precolonial Filipinos were exposed to Buddhist culture and religion through trade contacts with China and other Southeast Asian countries. This exposure led to the incorporation of some Buddhist elements in precolonial Philippine culture and religion.

Islam

Precolonial Filipinos converted to Islam through trade contacts with Arabia and Malaysia. The spread of Islam in the Philippines can be traced back to the 13th century, with the arrival of Arab and Malay Muslim traders in Mindanao.
