Prehistoric Period

The prehistoric period of the Philippines, which spans from around 250,000 BCE to 900 CE, was a time of significant human development and cultural growth. During this era, the earliest human settlements in the Philippines emerged, including those of the Tabon Man, the Callao Man, and the Angono Petroglyphs.

Early Historic Period

The early historic period of the Philippines, which spans from around 900 CE to 1300 CE, marked a significant turning point in the country's history. During this era, the emergence of writing and trade became increasingly prevalent in the Philippines, as evidenced by the Laguna Copperplate Inscription.

Classical Period

The classical period of the Philippines, which spans from around 1300 CE to 1500 CE, was a time of significant political, economic, social, and cultural development. During this era, various polities and kingdoms rose and fell in the precolonial Philippines, including Cebu, Butuan, Sulu, Maguindanao, Lanao, among others.

Late Historic Period

The late historic period of the Philippines, which spans from around 1500 CE to 1565 CE, was a time of significant changes such as the expansion of Islam in the region, which led to the establishment of Muslim sultanates in the southern Philippines.
